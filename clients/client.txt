## Thermabond concept proposal

**Project:** Thermabond brand identity refinement  
**Document Ref:** TB-BRD-BRF-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** July 2026  

Thermabond is seeking a refined identity that is simple, distinctive, premium, and highly functional across digital, print, vehicle signage, and embroidered workwear. The brief calls for a typographic-first solution with minimal visual elements, avoiding literal construction clichés and ensuring the mark remains legible at very small sizes and in one-colour reproduction. [pitch](https://pitch.com/templates/Design-Proposal-3S7kAq4HnHNf54eUCF2jyunD)

## Creative approach

I would recommend exploring **2–3 typographic directions** rather than forcing the identity into a single pure wordmark. The best route may be a refined wordmark, but an integrated symbol or hybrid combination mark should remain on the table if it strengthens recognition and usability. The design language should feel disciplined, modern, and production-safe, with an emphasis on clarity over ornament. [ixdf](https://ixdf.org/literature/topics/design-proposals)

The visual system should be built around strong letterforms, controlled spacing, and a small distinguishing device that can function as an ownable brand cue. That device should be simple enough to survive embroidery, favicons, and monochrome applications without breakdown. [agentsonly](https://www.agentsonly.com/brand-guideline-categories/application)

## Direction options

### Direction 1: Pure typographic refinement
A clean wordmark built from custom-drawn letterforms inspired by the existing DM Sans system, but adjusted for stronger personality and trademark distinctiveness. This direction would rely on proportion, spacing, and a subtle custom detail rather than illustration.

### Direction 2: Wordmark with device
A restrained wordmark paired with a compact geometric device that can stand alone at favicon size or as a stamp on workwear and equipment. The device would be designed to stay bold and legible when reduced for embroidery or single-colour print. [towardsemployment](https://www.towardsemployment.org/wp-content/uploads/TE_BrandGuidelines_Updated-4.17.25.pdf)

### Direction 3: Hybrid identity
A more integrated solution where one letterform is modified to create a brand cue inside the name itself. This would preserve simplicity while offering a stronger proprietary feel, provided the mark remains clear at small scale. [pitch](https://pitch.com/templates/Design-Proposal-3S7kAq4HnHNf54eUCF2jyunD)

## Production considerations

Because Thermabond expects heavy use on embroidered uniforms, hi-vis workwear, PPE, and vehicle signage, every direction should be tested in a simplified form. Fine strokes, tight counters, gradients, and decorative detail should be avoided, since these can fail in stitching and small reproduction. The final artwork should be prepared as fully editable vector files with clean outlines and straightforward color separations for reliable production. [agentsonly](https://www.agentsonly.com/brand-guideline-categories/application)

## Deliverables

Planned deliverables would include:
- 2–3 initial concept directions.
- One consolidated round of revisions.
- Final artwork in AI, EPS, and/or SVG.
- High-resolution PNG exports with transparent background.
- Primary colour, navy single-colour, reversed white, and monochrome black versions. [pitch](https://pitch.com/templates/Design-Proposal-3S7kAq4HnHNf54eUCF2jyunD)

## Suggested timeline

A practical turnaround for the initial concept round would be about **5–7 business days** after receiving confirmation of scope, access to reference material, and any production preferences. Final timing would depend on the speed of feedback and the complexity of refinement.

## Positioning note

This identity should avoid visual similarity to existing construction or asphalt-sector marks while still feeling credible in a trade and industrial environment. The goal is a mark that reads as premium and operationally robust, not decorative. [venngage](https://venngage.com/blog/proposal-design/)
