Logo Design Brief: Islamic Tarbiyah Association
We are seeking a professional 2D logo design for the Islamic Tarbiyah Association.

Main English name: Islamic Tarbiyah Association
Arabic name: جمعية التربية الإسلامية

The main focus for this competition is the English logo. An Arabic version is not mandatory at this stage, as we may develop it internally later. However, designers who can also provide a strong Arabic or bilingual version will have a higher chance of winning.


About the Association
The Islamic Tarbiyah Association is an established educational and community-based association rooted in Islamic values.

Its work focuses on education, tarbiyah, character development, community engagement, student support, cultural activities, teacher development, social care, and service to society.

The association is not seeking a logo that looks like a mosque, religious academy, Qur’an school, or Islamic studies institute. While Islamic values are central to the association’s identity, the logo should focus more broadly on education, values, nurturing, community, growth, and social contribution.


Desired Logo Style
The logo should feel:
- Modern
- Professional
- Educational
- Trustworthy
- Warm
- Community-focused
- Nurturing
- Dignified
- Values-based
- Culturally Islamic, but not overly religious

The logo should not feel:
- Political
- Harsh
- Outdated
- Overly decorative
- Generic
- Childish
- Charity-only
- Like a mosque or religious institute
- Design Direction

We are open to creative interpretation and encourage designers to explore original concepts.
The logo should represent the association as a values-based educational and community institution, not as a purely religious organisation.

The Islamic identity should be reflected through values such as dignity, balance, care, moderation, ethics, and community service, rather than through heavy religious imagery.

Please avoid making the main logo overly dependent on obvious religious symbols such as mosque domes, minarets, crescents, Qur’an stands, or heavy Islamic ornamentation.



Colours
Colours are open to designer creativity.

Suggested directions may include deep navy, warm gold/sand, soft teal, white/off-white, or other professional and timeless combinations suitable for an educational and community association.

The colour palette should feel credible, warm, professional, and suitable for long-term institutional use.



Typography
The English typography should be clean, professional, warm, and suitable for an educational/community institution.

If an Arabic version is provided, the Arabic typography should be clear, elegant, modern, and easy to read.

The English and Arabic versions, where provided, should feel like they belong to the same brand family.



Required Deliverables for Competition Stage
For this stage, please provide simple 2D logo submissions only.

Required:
Horizontal logo version
Stacked logo version
Standalone icon/symbol, if the design includes one
Basic colour palette
Basic font/typography recommendation

Preferred but not mandatory:
Arabic version
Bilingual version

Please do not submit 3D mockups, wall signs, stationery mockups, business card mockups, or presentation effects. We want to evaluate the logo clearly in a simple 2D format.



Final Note
The best design will be simple, memorable, professional, values-driven, and suitable for long-term institutional use.

The logo should help communicate education, tarbiyah, community, care, growth, and social contribution, with Islamic values present in a subtle and refined way.



Multiple Winners
This competition may have multiple winners, not just one, depending on the quality, originality, and suitability of the ideas and designs submitted.

We are open to awarding more than one designer if multiple strong concepts are presented.



What We Will Judge On- submissions will be judged based on:
- Professional, modern typography and spacing
- A strong and memorable visual concept
- Clean execution, not overly detailed
- Strong legibility and balance in 2D
- Works equally well in monochrome and in colour
- Suitable for a values-based educational and community association
- Originality and avoidance of generic logo clichés
- Ability to feel professional, warm, trustworthy, and long-term
- Appropriate balance between educational/community identity and Islamic values
