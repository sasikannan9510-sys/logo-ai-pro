# Company Name
Four Peaks Building

# Industry
Modern Rural Construction (Geraldine, New Zealand)

# Website Purpose
To establish Four Peaks Building as a premier, trusted, and professional modern rural construction company in New Zealand. To meticulously showcase their expertise, portfolio of high-quality, architecturally precise projects, and unique craftsmanship. To generate high-quality leads by providing clear and elegant calls-to-action for consultations and project inquiries. To communicate their brand personality of honesty, reliability, modern craftsmanship, and deep inspiration from the New Zealand landscape.

# Target Audience
*   **High-Net-Worth Individuals/Families:** Seeking custom-built, premium, architecturally significant rural homes or lifestyle properties.
*   **Property Developers & Investors:** Looking for a reliable and high-quality construction partner for bespoke rural residential or commercial developments.
*   **Architects & Designers:** Seeking collaborative partners who uphold exceptional standards of precision, craftsmanship, and design integrity.
*   **Rural Business Owners:** Requiring modern, durable, and aesthetically pleasing commercial, agricultural, or hospitality structures in rural settings.
*   **Anyone:** Who values exceptional quality, architectural precision, timeless design, and a professional, honest, and landscape-integrated approach to construction.

# Brand Personality
Honest, Rural, Premium, Professional, Reliable, Modern Craftsmanship, New Zealand Landscape Inspired, Precise, Timeless, Architectural.

# Design Direction
The design direction is a sophisticated, minimalist, and architecturally inspired digital experience. It must embody a "Swiss-inspired corporate identity" with a strong emphasis on clean lines, geometric precision, and an elegant sense of balance and proportion. The aesthetic should be modern, premium, timeless, and robust, reflecting the company's unwavering commitment to quality and durability in construction. Key principles include a typography-driven approach, generous use of negative space, a pristine information hierarchy, editorial-style layouts, and a profound, yet subtle, connection to the New Zealand landscape through high-quality photography and understated visual cues. The entire design should feel substantial, precise, and refined, deliberately avoiding any fleeting design trends or superficial elements.

# Visual Identity
The visual identity of the homepage will directly extend the "Swiss-inspired" and "Architectural precision" established in the Four Peaks Building logo.
*   **Geometric Forms:** Consistent and deliberate use of clean, geometric shapes and lines will define the layout, component design, and any graphical elements, subtly echoing the abstract mountain peaks in the brand's logo.
*   **Subtle Textures/Patterns:** Minimal and refined textures, such as faint concrete, natural wood grain, or sophisticated stone patterns, may be used sparingly in backgrounds or overlays to hint at premium building materials without overwhelming the clean, minimalist aesthetic.
*   **Iconography:** Icons will be line-based, purely geometric, and minimalist, maintaining consistency with the overall brand precision.
*   **Brand Mark Integration:** The Four Peaks Building combination mark and standalone icon will be prominently and consistently integrated, particularly in the header, footer, and as subtle brand markers throughout the page.
*   **Photography:** High-quality, professional photography is paramount, focusing on architectural details, completed projects, the use of natural and premium materials, and the majestic New Zealand landscape, forging a strong connection between the rural context and modern construction.

# Color Palette
*   **Primary Background:** Warm Cream (#F4F0E6) - The dominant background color, providing a soft, inviting, premium, and spacious foundation for all content, enhancing readability and elegance.
*   **Primary Text & Structure:** Charcoal Black (#222222) - Used for primary headings, body text, structural elements, and crisp outlines. Conveys strength, professionalism, and elegance.
*   **Primary Accent & CTA:** Moss Green (#5B6F47) - Utilized for primary call-to-action buttons, key highlights, interactive elements, and subtle graphical accents. Represents nature, growth, and the distinct New Zealand landscape connection.
*   **Secondary Accent (Optional):** Forest Green (#445C3C) - A slightly deeper, richer variant of green, used sparingly for secondary calls-to-action or subtle emphasis, adding depth and versatility to the natural palette.

# Typography
Typography is a foundational element, central to achieving the "premium," "architectural," and "typography-driven" aesthetic.
*   **Headings (H1-H6):** A custom architectural sans-serif font, bold, refined, and highly legible, reminiscent of typefaces like "Archivo Black," "Montserrat Semi-Bold/Bold," or "Neue Haas Grotesk Display." These should command attention with strong visual hierarchy, meticulous kerning, and precise tracking, embodying an architectural blueprint's clarity and elegance.
*   **Body Text:** A highly readable, clean, and neutral sans-serif font for paragraphs, detailed information, and descriptive text. Examples include "Inter," "Open Sans," or "Source Sans Pro." This typeface should recede slightly, allowing the headings and generous white space to define the architectural and editorial tone, while ensuring excellent legibility across all device sizes.
*   **Monospace/Numbers (Optional):** A subtle monospace font could be introduced for specific technical data points, code snippets, or precise numbering to inject an additional layer of technicality and precision.
*   **Weights & Styles:** A considered range of weights (Light, Regular, Medium, Semi-Bold, Bold) within the chosen font families should be utilized to create hierarchy and visual interest without introducing additional, conflicting font families.

# Layout Structure
The layout will be clean, meticulously structured, and editorial, prioritizing visual balance, clear information flow, and a substantial, premium feel, akin to a high-end architectural journal.
*   **Header / Navigation:**
    *   **Logo:** The "Four Peaks Building" combination mark, prominently positioned on the far left.
    *   **Primary Navigation:** Minimalist, clearly labeled links (e.g., Home, Services, Portfolio, About, Contact) aligned to the upper right.
    *   **Call-to-Action (CTA) Button:** A distinguished "Request a Quote" or "Contact Us" button, styled in Moss Green, integrated as the final element in the navigation, providing immediate access for inquiries.
    *   **Subtle Effects:** Minimalist hover states for navigation links, such as a subtle underline animation or a gentle text color shift.
*   **Hero Section:**
    *   **Visual Dominance:** A full-width, high-resolution, impactful photograph or a subtle, cinematic video loop showcasing a completed premium rural build, an exquisite architectural detail, or the iconic Four Peaks landscape bathed in natural light.
    *   **Headline:** A large, bold, architectural sans-serif headline conveying the company's core value proposition (e.g., "Crafting Enduring Legacies in New Zealand's Landscape"). Concise and impactful.
    *   **Subheadline:** A shorter, descriptive sentence in body text, elegantly expanding on the headline.
    *   **Primary CTA:** A prominent Moss Green button (e.g., "Explore Our Portfolio" or "Begin Your Project"), centrally located or aligned below the text.
    *   **Minimal Overlays:** Text should be perfectly legible against the visual, with subtle, non-intrusive overlay styling for contrast.
*   **Feature Sections (Services/Expertise):**
    *   **Grid Layout:** A clean, geometric grid (e.g., 2 or 3 columns) showcasing key service areas (e.g., Custom Rural Homes, Commercial & Agricultural Structures, Renovations & Extensions).
    *   **Card-based:** Each service presented within a clean, elegant card, featuring a compelling image or minimalist icon, a concise title, a brief description, and a "Learn More" link.
    *   **Focus:** Emphasize the unique craftsmanship, architectural precision, and bespoke nature of their work.
*   **Content Sections (About Us / Our Philosophy):**
    *   **Editorial Style:** Characterized by generous white space, large typography for impactful pull quotes, and meticulously structured paragraphs.
    *   **Narrative:** A compelling narrative about Four Peaks Building's journey, their unwavering commitment to modern craftsmanship, their profound connection to the New Zealand landscape, and their core values of honesty and reliability.
    *   **Visual Integration:** May include a striking portrait of the founder, a high-quality team photograph, or an abstract architectural illustration.
*   **Portfolio / Key Projects Section:**
    *   **Visual Showcase:** A highly curated selection of 3-5 high-impact project images, presented in an elegant and spacious manner.
    *   **Clean Presentation:** Each project displayed with a large image, a minimalist title overlay, and a clear "View Project" CTA.
    *   **Grid or Masonry:** A flexible grid or masonry layout to highlight the diversity and architectural excellence of completed projects.
*   **Call-to-Action Section:**
    *   **Dedicated & Prominent:** A focused, full-width section with a subtle Warm Cream or Moss Green background tint, featuring a clear, inspiring headline (e.g., "Ready to Transform Your Vision into Reality?").
    *   **Strong CTA:** A large, contrasting Moss Green button (e.g., "Schedule a Consultation").
    *   **Minimal Supporting Text:** A concise, persuasive sentence reinforcing the call to action.
*   **Testimonials Section:**
    *   **Credibility Building:** Displaying impactful, authentic quotes from satisfied clients.
    *   **Clean Cards/Carousel:** Each testimonial presented in a simple, elegant card format, potentially with a client photo (if available and consented) and name/project details.
    *   **Subtle Navigation:** If a carousel, simple, clean navigation arrows.
*   **Footer:**
    *   **Comprehensive & Structured:** Divided into clear, well-spaced columns.
    *   **Navigation:** Repeat key navigational links for ease of access.
    *   **Contact Information:** Full address, phone numbers, and email.
    *   **Social Media Icons:** Minimalist, geometric icons linked to relevant professional platforms.
    *   **Legal:** Copyright information, privacy policy, terms of service.
    *   **Brand Mark:** A small, subtle Four Peaks icon or wordmark for brand reinforcement.
    *   **Color:** Could utilize a Charcoal Black background with Warm Cream text for strong contrast, or continue the Warm Cream background for a lighter feel.

# Grid System
*   **12-Column Grid:** A robust and highly flexible 12-column grid system will serve as the foundational structure for precise alignment, consistent spacing, and flawless responsive design. This ensures that all content blocks, imagery, and typographic elements are meticulously organized, scaled, and presented.
*   **Gutters:** Consistent and generous gutter widths (e.g., 24px or 32px) will be implemented to enhance the sense of spaciousness, promote visual separation between elements, and contribute to the "large white space" aesthetic.
*   **Margins:** Clearly defined page margins will create a distinct content container, preventing elements from touching the screen edges and reinforcing the editorial, architectural feel of the layout.
*   **Baseline Grid (Subtle):** A subtle underlying baseline grid will be utilized to ensure vertical rhythm and typographic harmony, particularly for body text, further contributing to the overall architectural precision.

# Spacing System
*   **8-Point Grid System:** All vertical and horizontal spacing (margins, padding, component sizing, and icon dimensions) will rigorously adhere to an 8-point base unit (multiples of 8px: 8px, 16px, 24px, 32px, 40px, 48px, 64px, 80px, 96px, 128px, etc.). This systematic approach guarantees consistency, visual harmony, and scalability across the entire design.
*   **Large White Space:** A paramount principle, generous white space will be intentionally integrated between sections, content blocks, and UI elements. This deliberate use of negative space creates a serene, premium, and sophisticated user experience, allowing elements to breathe, command attention, and contribute significantly to the "editorial layout" and "architectural" feel.

# Components
*   **Buttons:**
    *   **Primary:** Solid Moss Green background (#5B6F47), Warm Cream text (#F4F0E6). Minimal border-radius (e.g., 4px) for a subtle architectural touch, or sharp 0px radius for maximum precision. Robust padding ensures a substantial feel. Subtle hover effect: slight darkening of the background or a very gentle 1-2px vertical lift with a soft, understated shadow.
    *   **Secondary:** Outline button with a Moss Green 1px border, transparent or Warm Cream background, and Moss Green text. Similar subtle hover effect.
    *   **Text Link:** Moss Green text, with a subtle underline animation that expands on hover.
    *   **Focus:** Clean, highly legible, and clear affordance for interactivity.
*   **Cards:**
    *   **Design:** Clean, minimalist design with defined, thin borders (Charcoal Black 1px) or very subtle shadows (`--shadow-s`) to create separation. Warm Cream background.
    *   **Content:** Typically includes a compelling image (top or side), a clear headline (H3/H4), concise body text, and a discreet "Learn More" link or button.
    *   **Spacing:** Generous internal padding and margin to maintain white space.
*   **Forms:**
    *   **Input Fields:** Clean, understated design featuring a subtle 1px Charcoal Black border and a Warm Cream background. Clear, top-aligned labels in Charcoal Black. Focus state visually indicated with a subtle Moss Green border highlight.
    *   **Error States:** Clear yet unobtrusive visual cues (e.g., thin red border, subtle error text) without being jarring.
    *   **Simplicity:** Minimalist styling throughout, prioritizing usability, clarity, and architectural precision.
*   **Icons:**
    *   **Style:** Purely line-based, geometric, and minimalist. Consistent stroke weight across all icons.
    *   **Purpose:** To support content, emphasize features, or serve as intuitive navigation elements (e.g., social media icons, service descriptors).
    *   **Color:** Charcoal Black or Moss Green, adapting dynamically for optimal contrast against various backgrounds.
*   **Dividers:**
    *   **Subtle Lines:** Thin (1px), charcoal black or dark grey lines used sparingly and strategically to visually delineate content sections where white space alone is insufficient.
    *   **White Space:** Primarily, generous white space will serve as the most prominent divider between sections, reinforcing the minimalist and spacious aesthetic.

# Imagery Style
*   **Photography:** High-resolution, professional, and artfully composed photography is paramount and non-negotiable.
    *   **Subject Matter:** Photography must meticulously showcase completed Four Peaks Building projects—capturing exterior architectural forms, intricate interior craftsmanship, the exquisite use of natural and premium materials, and the seamless integration of structures within the picturesque rural New Zealand landscape.
    *   **Mood & Tone:** Emphasize natural light, authenticity, a profound sense of permanence, and sophisticated calm. Images should convey the robust quality and timeless elegance of the builds. Avoid any overly filtered, artificial-looking, or generic stock photography.
    *   **Landscape Integration:** Images should frequently and naturally feature the stunning New Zealand natural environment (e.g., majestic mountains, rolling hills, native flora, dramatic skies), powerfully reinforcing the company's rural identity and landscape-inspired brand.
    *   **People (if present):** If individuals are depicted, they should be shown interacting with the spaces in a natural, unobtrusive manner, conveying comfort, quality of life, and the inherent value of the built environment.
*   **Illustration (Minimal):** If illustrations are utilized, they must be extremely minimalist, geometric, line-based, and abstract, directly echoing the clean style of the Four Peaks logo. They should serve as subtle complements, never distracting from the primary focus on photography and the architectural aesthetic. Perhaps for simplified process diagrams or unique icons.
*   **Abstract Visuals:** Very subtle, non-intrusive textured overlays or faint geometric patterns (e.g., delicate grids, abstract representations of premium building materials like honed concrete, timber grain, or natural stone) might be employed sparingly in backgrounds or as elegant compositional elements to add depth and sophistication without introducing visual clutter.

# Interaction & Animation
Interactions and animations will be subtle, refined, and highly purposeful, meticulously crafted to enhance the premium, architectural feel without ever becoming distracting, flashy, or superfluous.
*   **Hover Effects:**
    *   **Buttons:** A slight, elegant darkening of the Moss Green background or a very subtle 1-2px vertical lift accompanied by a soft, understated shadow.
    *   **Navigation Links:** A precise, subtle underline animation that expands smoothly on hover, or a gentle, sophisticated text color shift.
    *   **Cards/Images:** A very subtle scale-up (e.g., 1.02x) or a soft, expanding border/shadow effect to clearly indicate interactivity and highlight content.
*   **Transitions:**
    *   **Page Transitions:** Smooth, fast fades or subtle, elegant horizontal slides for a seamless experience (relevant for single-page applications or fluid internal navigation).
    *   **Section Reveals:** Gentle fade-ins or subtle slide-ups for content sections as they elegantly enter the viewport during scrolling, creating a refined and intentional content reveal.
*   **Micro-interactions:**
    *   **Form Fields:** A clean, crisp border color change or a subtle shadow on focus to guide user input.
    *   **Checkboxes/Radio Buttons:** Custom-styled, minimal, with a satisfying and visually clear confirmation on selection.
    *   **Loading States:** Minimalist, geometrically styled animated spinner or a clean progress bar that perfectly aligns with the brand's architectural aesthetic.
    *   **Scroll Indicators:** A subtle progress bar at the top of the page indicating scroll depth, or a clean, minimalist scroll-to-top button that appears on demand.

# Mobile Responsive Design
The desktop design will be meticulously adapted to provide a flawless and premium experience across all mobile devices (tablets and smartphones), zealously preserving the architectural aesthetic, clean information hierarchy, and professional brand integrity.
*   **Content Prioritization:** Essential information, key calls-to-action, and core value propositions will be strategically prioritized and made immediately accessible and prominent on smaller screens.
*   **Navigation:**
    *   **Hamburger Menu:** The desktop header will elegantly transform into a minimalist hamburger icon (Charcoal Black) on mobile.
    *   **Off-Canvas/Full-Screen Menu:** Tapping the hamburger icon will reveal a clean, full-screen overlay or an off-canvas menu with large, clearly labeled navigation links, maintaining typography consistency and ease of use.
*   **Layout Adaptation:**
    *   **Stacking:** Multi-column layouts (e.g., feature sections, portfolio grids) will fluidly and intelligently stack vertically into single columns on smaller screens.
    *   **Image Scaling:** All imagery will scale down proportionally to fit the screen width, meticulously maintaining aspect ratios and visual quality.
    *   **Text Scaling:** Typography will adapt to appropriate, highly readable sizes for mobile, ensuring optimal legibility without sacrificing the distinct architectural feel or visual impact.
*   **Touch Optimization:** Generous tap targets will be implemented for all buttons and links, precisely optimized for comfortable finger interaction.
*   **Performance:** All images and assets will be rigorously optimized for faster loading times on mobile networks, ensuring a swift and seamless user experience.
*   **White Space:** While maintaining a sense of openness, white space might be subtly condensed on mobile to optimize screen real estate while vigilantly avoiding any sense of clutter or visual compromise.
